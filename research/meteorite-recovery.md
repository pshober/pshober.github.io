## The full chain, end to end

Orbit-bearing meteorites — samples whose pre-atmospheric orbit we know — are the
gold standard for linking laboratory geochemistry back to the dynamics of the solar
system. Getting one means running the whole chain: multi-station astrometry and
photometry, triangulation, velocities and orbits, ablation/fragmentation and
dark-flight modelling, and finally boots on the ground. I work on this across three
**wide-field fireball networks**: the Desert Fireball Network (DFN, my PhD), the
**Global Fireball Observatory** (GFO — 18 institutions across 9 countries), and
**FRIPON** (15 nations, four continents), where I've been part of the scientific core
team since 2022.

## Pipelines you can trust

I led the first systematic **inter-network comparison of bolide reduction pipelines**,
reprocessing a large FRIPON sample of the Geminids to quantify the methodological
differences between four of the world's major meteor-physics groups (Canada, France,
Australia, Slovakia). It surfaced concrete biases — a systematic right-ascension
offset from epoch/frame handling, and a typical over-estimate of initial velocity
when deceleration is poorly constrained — and produced recommendations that have
**already led to fixes in FRIPON**, while providing a template for future multi-network
analyses.

## Recovered falls

I've contributed data reduction and analysis to a series of recovered and
high-profile falls:

- **Winchcombe** (CM chondrite) — rapid orbit determination enabled recovery of
  0.53 kg of pristine, minimally-altered carbonaceous material.
- **2023 CX1** — one of the few asteroids detected *before* impact, which dropped
  meteorites over Normandy.
- **Arpu Kuilpu** — an H5 ordinary chondrite traced to the outer main belt.
- **Madura Cave** — trajectory, recovery and orbital history of an Australian fall.

To raise recovery rates I also help develop **drone- and machine-learning-assisted
search** methods, generalising techniques proven in the Australian outback to varied
terrain, and coordinate field campaigns with citizen-science partners such as
**Vigie-Ciel**.
